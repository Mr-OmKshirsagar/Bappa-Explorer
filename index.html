Cities

Pune

Shrimant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati

The majestic Shreemant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati stands as Pune’s pride, where devotion meets grandeur. Its golden-adorned idol is a timeless symbol of faith, unity, and celebration.

Kasba Ganapati

Kasba Ganpati, the gramdaivat of Pune, is revered as the city’s guardian. Known for its deep-rooted tradition, it leads the Ganeshotsav processions with unmatched pride and devotion.

Tambdi Jogeshwari Ganpati

Tambdi Jogeshwari Ganpati, one of Pune’s oldest deities, is worshipped as the fierce yet protective goddess of the city. Its vibrant red idol embodies strength, tradition, and unshaken faith of generations.

Mumbai

Lalbaugcha Raja

Lalbaugcha Raja, Mumbai’s most iconic Ganpati, is hailed as the Navsacha Raja (King of Wishes). Millions flock each year, believing that every prayer made at his feet finds divine fulfillment.

Mumbai Cha Raja

Mumbai cha Raja, a beloved Ganpati of the city, is celebrated for its grandeur and deep-rooted faith. Known as the people’s king, it radiates devotion, hope, and togetherness across Mumbai.

Andheri Cha Maharaja

Andheri Cha Maharaja, one of Mumbai’s most famous Ganpati idols, is cherished for its grandeur and community spirit. Known as the “Navsacha Raja,” it is believed to fulfill the heartfelt wishes of devotees.

Nagpur

Shree Ganesh Mandir Tekdi

Tekdi Ganpati of Nagpur, perched on a hillock, is among the city’s oldest and most revered temples. Its serene idol is a beacon of faith, drawing devotees seeking peace, strength, and blessings.

Mahal Cha Raja

Nagpur's Mahal Cha Raja is a historic Ganpati idol with deep ties to the royal Bhonsle family. It is famed for its traditional grandeur, attracting immense crowds of worshippers seeking blessings.

Nagpur Cha Raja

Nagpur Cha Raja, located in Reshimbagh, is a prominent Ganpati revered as a wish-fulfilling deity. It is celebrated for its magnificent idol and grand-scale festivities that attract thousands of devotees for darshan.
